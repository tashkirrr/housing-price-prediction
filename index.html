What Drives California Home Prices?

Understanding the key factors that influence property values across the Golden State.

Median Income

The strongest predictor of home prices. Neighborhoods with higher median incomes consistently show elevated property values, reflecting the economic health of the community.

Ocean Proximity

Properties near the ocean or bay command significant premiums. Island properties average 84% higher prices than inland homes.

Location Matters

Distance to major cities like San Francisco and Los Angeles significantly impacts values. The Bay Area and coastal regions remain the most expensive markets.

Room Density

More rooms per household indicates more spacious, valuable properties. This metric often correlates with overall home quality and desirability.

About the Model

This predictor uses a HistGradientBoostingRegressor trained on 20,640 California census records from 1990. The model achieves an R² score of 0.85, meaning it explains 85% of price variation. We engineered additional features like distance to major cities and room ratios to improve accuracy.

Tips for Accurate Predictions

Income Correlation

Higher median income in a neighborhood typically translates to higher home values. This is our model's strongest predictor.

Coastal Premium

Properties near the ocean or bay are generally more expensive. The "Island" category commands the highest premiums.

Space Matters

More rooms per household indicates more valuable properties. Consider this ratio when evaluating homes.

Historical Context

The model uses 1990 data. For current values, multiply predictions by approximately 2.5 to account for inflation.
